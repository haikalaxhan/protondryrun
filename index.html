⚠️ This Was a Phishing Simulation

You have just interacted with a simulated phishing login page created for cybersecurity awareness training.

The link you accessed from the event invitation email was not a real registration system. It was a controlled security exercise by Corporate IT Security.

🔍 What This Simulation Tested

This exercise tested your response to a fake login page requesting corporate credentials.

  • Company username
  • Company password

Attackers often use fake login pages to steal credentials and gain unauthorized access to corporate systems.

🔐 Protect Your Credentials — Verify Before You Login

  • Attackers can capture your username and password immediately when you enter them into a fake login page.
  • Credential theft does not require MFA to be present—once credentials are entered, they can be reused instantly by attackers.
  • Always verify the full URL and domain name before entering any login information.
  • Attackers may also create fake MFA prompts or fake verification pages to trick users into entering authentication codes.
  • Never enter MFA codes, approve prompts, or provide verification numbers unless you are 100% sure the request comes from a legitimate system.

🔍 Key Phishing Indicators

  • 🎁 Social Engineering: Attractive event invitation used as bait.
  • ⏳ Urgency: Limited time or exclusivity pressure.
  • 🏆 Brand Impersonation: Fake corporate communication style.
  • 🔐 Fake Login Page: Credential request on a non-official system.
  • 🌐 Domain Spoofing: Email appears legitimate but uses a lookalike domain.
