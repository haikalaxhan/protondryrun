Company username Company password Attackers often use fake login pages to steal credentials and gain unauthorized access to corporate systems. 🔐 Protect Your Credentials — Verify Before You Login Attackers can capture your username and password immediately when you enter them into a fake login page. Credential theft does not require MFA to be present—once credentials are entered, they can be reused instantly by attackers. Always verify the full URL and domain name before entering any login information. Attackers may also create fake MFA prompts or fake verification pages to trick users into entering authentication codes. Never enter MFA codes, approve prompts, or provide verification numbers unless you are 100% sure the request comes from a legitimate system. 🔍 Key Phishing Indicators 🎁 Social Engineering: Attractive event invitation used as bait. ⏳ Urgency: Limited time or exclusivity pressure. 🏆 Brand Impersonation: Fake corporate communication style. 🔐 Fake Login Page: Credential request on a non-official system. 🌐 Domain Spoofing: Email appears legitimate but uses a lookalike domain. Important Lesson: Always verify the sender domain. In this simulation, the email appeared as: Display Name: PetronMY-Announcement Sender Address: Petron-Announcement@petron.corm.my The domain contains a subtle typo (corm.my instead of com.my). Attackers often rely on users checking only the display name and not the full email address. 🎓 Why This Matters Credential phishing remains one of the most common attack methods in cyber incidents. Stolen credentials can lead to serious security risks, including: 🔐 Unauthorized system access 🔐 Data leakage 🔐 Account takeover attempts Thank you for supporting cybersecurity awareness.
