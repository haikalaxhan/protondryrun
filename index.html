You have been targeted as part of an authorized security awareness exercise conducted by the IT Security team.

What happened?

You received a simulated phishing email that mimicked a Microsoft account security alert. The link in that email led you to a credential harvesting page designed to look like a legitimate Office 365 sign-in screen.

What should you do next time?

  • Check the sender's full email address, not just the display name
  • Hover over links before clicking — verify they point to a legitimate domain
  • Look for urgency cues like "verify now" or "your account will be locked"
  • Report suspicious emails to security@yourdomain.com
  • Never enter credentials from a link in an email — navigate to the site directly

Red flags you might have missed

  • Generic greeting instead of your name
  • Suspicious sender domain (@netwitz.com.my is not a Microsoft domain)
  • URL pointed to login.haikalaxhan.site instead of microsoft.com
  • Created sense of urgency and fear
